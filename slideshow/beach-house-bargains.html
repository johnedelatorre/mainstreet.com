Great Beach House Bargains

  • Mark David
  • Jul 30, 2010 7:00 AM EDT

  • Beach Bargains

    Some of the most expensive property in all of the U.S. is clustered along a few stretches of sand that almost no on can afford. In the Hamptons, the bottom of the market for an oceanfront single family home exceeds $5 million. In Malibu, a small house sitting on the sand will set someone back a minimum of $2 million and the market climbs steeply from there to more than $20 million. But what if the budget for a beach house, a real honest to goodness waterfront house in a beach community tops out at $500,000? Can it still be done? The answer is yes, but budget-minded beach house buyers have to be willing to go out of the most expensive beach towns to be able to set down roots in the sand. Photo Credit: Windermere Real Estate
    Washington State Waterfront

  • Washington State Waterfront

    The rugged southern Pacific coastline of Washington State is every bit as dramatic and picturesque as any of the more expensive oceanfront locales on the West Coast. Just outside Westport, Wash., on an 18-mile stretch of sugary sand sits an ocean front cottage and guest house listed at $465,000. Photo Credit: Windermere Real Estate
    Washington State Waterfront

  • Washington State Waterfront

    A long drive snakes through the sea grass to the recently built two-bedroom and two-bathroom Craftsman style cottage where a wind-protected garden at the front of the house separates the main house from the detached guest house. Photo Credit: Windermere Real Estate
    Washington State Waterfront

  • Washington State Waterfront

    The open plan main living space has gigantic windows with panoramic views, hardwood flooring, vaulted ceiling, built-in seating and a wood burning stove for those foggy mornings and nippy seaside nights. Photo Credit: Windermere Real Estate
    Washington State Waterfront

  • Washington State Waterfront

    The small but functional and fully equipped kitchen is open to the living/dining room and includes granite counter tops, maple cabinetry, large pantry cabinet, dishwasher and a dining built-in banquet with ocean views. Photo Credit: Windermere Real Estate
    Washington State Waterfront

  • Washington State Waterfront

    In addition to the detached, ocean view guest house fitted with a sink, microwave and three-quarter bathroom, there are wide decks that wrap around the ocean side of the house for taking in the view across the waving sea grass to the ocean. The side yard is plumbed for a built-in hot tub and a private path leads from the deck to the shoreline. Photo Credit: Windermere Real Estate
    South of the Border Beach Bargain

  • South of the Border Beach Bargain

    Beach house bargain hunters might consider something out of the country but still easily accessible. In the Mexican seaside resort town of Rosarito Beach, located on the Pacific Coast just 20 miles south of the border, a nearly new 1,850 square-foot ocean front house in a secured community in the Baja del Mar area is currently listed at $345,000. Photo Credit: Baja 123
    South of the Border Beach Bargain

  • South of the Border Beach Bargain

    There are three bedrooms and two bathrooms plus a large, open plan living space with massive central fireplace, tall arched windows and a huge kitchen with tile counter tops and a huge work island and breakfast bar. A large terrace with stone balustrade hangs over the beach below and offers unobstructed views up and down the beach, of the Coronado Islands, and the migrating dolphins that make their way up the Pacific Coast each year. For more information contact Baja 123 at (760) 203-2227 or (888) 483-5873. Photo Credit: Baja 123
    Pensacola Beach Modern

  • Pensacola Beach Modern

    While Floridas Miami Beach and Palm Beach get most of the attention, the Florida panhandle also has miles and miles of pristine beaches on the Gulf of Mexico. In Pensacola Beach, a long and narrow sandbar that protects the Pensacola Bay, an almost 2,000 square-foot house with 100 feet of bay frontage is currently listed at $499,000. Photo Credit: Levin & Rinke Resort Realty
    Pensacola Beach Modern

  • Pensacola Beach Modern

    The four-bedroom and two-bathroom cedar sided contemporary house in the Sugar Bowl neighborhood sits on nearly half an acre with panoramic views to the north, east and west. In addition to the living room that has beamed ceiling and wood paneled walls, the main living areas include a dining room and a large kitchen with plenty of room to cook for an army of weekend guests. Photo Credit: Levin & Rinke Resort Realty
    Pensacola Beach Modern

  • Pensacola Beach Modern

    The back of the house opens onto a multi-level deck with built-in seating, expansive views of the bay and easy access to the water and sandy bay side beach. The wide sandy Gulf-side beach is just a two-block walk. Photo Credit: Levin & Rinke Resort Realty
    Giddyup to the Beach in Galveston

  • Giddyup to the Beach in Galveston

    The great state of Texas may not be situated on the Atlantic or the Pacific, but it still has hundreds of miles of scenic coastline on the Gulf of Mexico. Just an hour outside of Houston on the slim spit of land known as Galveston Island, a water front house with three bedrooms and two bathrooms listed at $499,000 sits high off the ground on stilts in the event hurricanes or high tides bring the Gulf waters up and over the sandy beach. Photo Credit: Prudential / Gary Greene Realtors
    Giddyup to the Beach in Galveston

  • Giddyup to the Beach in Galveston

    The interior spaces include a great room with cathedral ceilings, a living area with fireplace, a dining area and a fully equipped kitchen with copious cabinets and counterspace. A loft area accessed by a spiral stair brings the sleeping capacity of the house to 14 making plenty of room for overflow family and friends. Photo Credit: Prudential / Gary Greene Realtors
    Giddyup to the Beach in Galveston

  • Giddyup to the Beach in Galveston

    The great room, which spans the entire width of the house, opens to a wide rear deck with unobstructed views of the beach and Gulf. An outdoor stair leads down the to carport and single-car enclosed garage as well as the beach. Photo Credit: Prudential / Gary Greene Realtors
    The Beach Grass is Greener in Greenport

  • The Beach Grass is Greener in Greenport

    New Yorkers with an itch for a house at the beach have it particularly hard financially speaking. The nearest ocean side communities in New Jersey, Connecticut and Long Island are prohibitively expensive for all but the wealthy, making this seasonal cottage on the far East End of the North Fork a surprising and honest to goodness bargain at $289,000. Photo Credit: Daniel Gale, Sothebys International Realty
    The Beach Grass is Greener in Greenport

  • The Beach Grass is Greener in Greenport

    This two-bedroom and one-bathroom cottage, located in a private co-operative community called Breezy Shores, is 90 miles due east from Midtown Manhattan and just outside the maritime village of Greenport, N.Y. The gated enclave includes 30-some other cottages from the 1920s and 1930s that line up along the shore of the Great Peconic Bay with views across to Shelter Island and the Hamptons. Photo Credit: Daniel Gale, Sothebys International Realty
    The Beach Grass is Greener in Greenport

  • The Beach Grass is Greener in Greenport

    The Breezy Shores community spreads across 90 waterfront acres of private property, and residents have use of a sports field, wide swathes of lawn, storage sheds, a private marina and a sandy beach that stretches as far as the eye can see. For more information contact Kristen Rishe or Sheila Izzo (631)734-5439 at Daniel Gale Sothebys International Realty in Cutchogue, N.Y. Photo Credit: Daniel Gale, Sothebys International Realty
