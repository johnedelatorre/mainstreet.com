Summer House Shares Bring Great Savings, But Watch Out

  • Lauren Lyons Cole
  • Jul 5, 2014 11:59 AM EDT
ADVERTISEMENT


WATCH: More personal finance videos on TheStreet TV | More videos from Juliette Fairley

NEW YORK (MainStreet) — Sharing a vacation rental with friends or family is a great way to keep costs down, but traveling together can put stress on the relationship and potentially spoil an otherwise relaxing vacation.

If you're hoping for a drama-free house share this summer, here are a few things to keep in mind.

Designate Roles

Designating roles ahead of time can go a long way toward avoiding confusion. Pick someone in to be in charge of coordinating communication, collecting payments and keeping track of the house keys. Or if it's easier, divide those responsibilities throughout the group.

Split the Cost Fairly

House shares rarely have equal bedroom and bathroom configurations, so spend some time figuring out who will sleep where, and how much they'll pay. The tiny attic bedroom should obviously be cheaper than the master bedroom, but the exact price breakdown is something you should all decide together.

Discuss Chores

There's no need to create a detailed cleaning schedule, but you should definitely reach a housekeeping agreement at the beginning of your vacation. It's worth it to discuss even the most basic of chores, like how long dishes will be left in the sink. There might be things that don't bother you but drive your friends absolutely nuts and vice versa. Better to identify those things ahead of time.

Budget for Food and Drinks

Splitting a grocery bill evenly may be the easiest way to share food costs, but it's not really the most fair and it can end up breeding resentment. If buying your own food doesn't seem like a good solution, consider other ideas, like taking turns buying and cooking meals. That way if someone is on a stricter budget they can still contribute while keeping the bill to something they can afford.

Appreciate Differences

Chances are your friends won't all have the same vacation routines you do. Meal and bedtime schedules may vary, and it's important to be considerate. Same thing goes for activities. It's a vacation, if you want to go hiking, go hiking. But if someone else wants to sit by the pool and catch up on reading, give that person the space to do that.
