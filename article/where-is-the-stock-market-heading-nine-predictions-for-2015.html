Where is the Stock Market Heading? Nine Predictions for 2015

  • Hal Bundrick
  • Dec 11, 2014 1:33 PM EST
ADVERTISEMENT

NEW YORK (MainStreet) — With the S&P 500 up 11% for the year to date, and considering last year's remarkable 32% gain, it's understandable that investors are puzzled by what's to come. As the end of this year approaches, the market analysts are making their predictions. Goldman Sachs expects the S&P 500 to end next year at just 2,100 – only 50 points or so from where we are now. Wells Fargo is much more bullish, looking for a 9% gain. But with a 200% return since bottoming out in March of 2009, there are few market watchers expecting a roaring bull market in 2015.

Merrill Lynch analysts are "upbeat but not exuberant," saying in a report, "Strong fundamentals and healthy growth in the U.S. economy support a case for investor optimism and opportunism; however, in the lower-return, higher-volatility environment projected ahead, selective allocation and defensive portfolio moves will be crucial for performance."

Call that cautiously optimistic.

“While our key measures suggest that the bull market in equities can continue, the sentiment is far from euphoric,” said Candace Browning, head of BofA Merrill Lynch Global Research. “The world appears to be under-allocated to stocks, and we believe we are still only a third of the way into the Great Rotation from bonds. In the current environment, now is the time for investors to be highly selective and make tactical moves to position portfolios for more thematic investing in a transforming world.”

The firm has a positive outlook for employment in the U.S. along with growing employment and an improving housing market. The Bank of America Merrill Lynch research team listed ten market predictions for 2015:
  • An S&P 500 2015 target of 2200, or about a 6% gain for the year
  • An accelerating U.S. economy with GDP growth of 3.3%. Global financial growth will be even slightly better: 3.7%
  • Emerging markets should expand by 4.5% next year, bolstered by lower energy prices and rebounding economies in countries such as Brazil and India.
  • Tame inflation, hovering around 1.5% in the U.S. However, deflationary concerns loom for Europe which "could trigger another debt crisis."
  • Continued low oil prices, but higher volatility. Merrill forecasts Brent crude oil at an average of $77 per barrel.
  • The firm expects gold prices to end the year lower, to $1,100 per ounce.
  • The bond market will see more investor outflows and buyers searching for quality. "U.S. investment-grade bonds could see a total return of zero," the analysts say, with meager returns worldwide. High yield bonds will finish the year with total returns of 6% in Asia and the emerging markets, around 5% in Europe and 2-3% in the U.S.
  • The outlook for the U.S. housing market is favorable, as the firm expects new existing home sales to rise 5% in 2015 and 3.2% in 2016, while rising home prices continue to slow.
  • Energy production will ease due to price sensitivity. Shale producers will suffer from a steep drop in prices with output dropping to nearly half of this year's production. "Both the U.S. natural gas and thermal coal markets are expected to remain weak throughout 2015, in our view, and liquid natural gas should enter a bear market," the report says.
