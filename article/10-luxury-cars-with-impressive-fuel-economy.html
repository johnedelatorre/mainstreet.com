10 Luxury Cars With Impressive Fuel Economy

  • Jason Notte
  • Oct 5, 2015 4:35 PM EDT
ADVERTISEMENT

NEW YORK (MainStreet) – Luxury and efficiency once existed on polar opposite ends of the automotive spectrum. Now, their fates are as entwined as the spaghetti-bowl lanes of an interstate interchange.

A luxury car likely isn't the surest sign that a driver cares about meeting the Environmental Protection Agency's goal of a fleet's combined 54.5 miles per gallon by 2025. The University of Michigan Transportation Research Institute found that cars and light trucks purchased in 2014 got an average of 25.4 miles to the gallon, and luxury vehicles tend to have mileage well south of that figure. Of the more than 220 vehicles identified as luxury sedans by the EPA between the 2015 and 2016 model years, roughly two dozen manage more than 25 miles per gallon combined.

High-end luxury vehicles including the Bentley Mulsanne (just 13 miles per gallon combined), the Rolls Royce Phantom (14 mpg), the Mercedes-Benz Maybach S600 (15 mpg) and BMW 760Li (15 mpg) aren't helping. However, even somewhat less-swank options like the Mercedes-Benz CLS500 (24 mpg) and BMW 328i (26 mpg) aren't all that thrifty with their fuel.

This isn't great news for luxury automakers, who've seen sales slump 1.8% year-to-date, according to MotorIntelligence. While cost is always going to keep the luxury sector's 747,000 cars sold through August a small percentage of the 5.3 million cars sold in the U.S. during that timeframe, changing consumer patterns are having an impact on sales.

Through August, Jaguar, Maserati and Bentley have all seen sales decline by roughly 4% apiece. Audi's total sales are up 12% so far, but car sales are near flat at a 0.5% uptick. Mercedes-Benz, meanwhile, has seen sales climb 8%, though its cars have only received a 1.1% boost. In fact, BMW's 7.1% boost in car sales is an anomaly in its class, with only Tesla's 6.1% gain coming anywhere close.

The luxury sedan is changing, and some automakers are far more prepared for it than others. With help from the EPA's FuelEconomy.gov site, we compiled a list of the most fuel-efficient luxury sedans on the market. The BMW i3 didn't make the cut because, well, it's tiny and not really a sedan, but its electric efficiency would still put it well ahead of most of the cars on this list:



10. 2015 Jaguar XF

MSRP: $51,900

Miles per gallon: 20 city, 30 highway, 25 combined

That doesn't look like a whole lot of efficiency, but when you consider that it's fueling a 3.0-liter V6 engine that generates between 340 and 380 horsepower, it's almost miraculous.

Coming to a stop automatically shuts down the engine, which restarts again as soon as you lift the brake pedal. Leather seating, Rosewood veneer, a 7-inch touchscreen, voice-controlled information and entertainment systems are all tasty little carrots for folks who need a little more than a break on gas prices to get behind the wheel of a luxury vehicle.

9. 2016 BMW 528i

MSRP: $58,700

Miles per gallon: 23 city, 34 highway, 28.5 combined

O.K., so maybe the 2.0-liter BMW four-cylinder engine and its 240 horsepower are a little pokey even with the turbocharger.

That isn't really the point of this vehicle. The hybrid-style brake energy regeneration system powering Xenon headlights, LED accent and fog lights, LCD displays, automatic dual-zone climate control, ten-speaker stereo system and moonroof is what's helping this vehicle's case. The leather steering wheel, dark wood trim, BMW Navigation and iDrive system with touchpad controller just make it a cushier ride.

8. 2016 Audi A6/A7 quattro

MSRP: $46,200 and 68,300

Miles per gallon: 25 city, 38 highway, 29.5 combined

They're just different sizes of the same diesel-driven car, so don't freak out about their options. Both vehicles get the access to the same features, including the Style package (xenon headlights and 18-inch wheels), the Innovation package (includes head-up display and night vision assistant), the Cold Weather package (heated front and rear seats and heated steering wheel), the Sport package (adds a sport-tuned suspension, a sport steering wheel, shift paddles and either 19-inch wheels with all-season tires or 20-inch wheels with summer tires), the Driver Assistance package (top-view camera system with front and rear corner views, adaptive cruise control, active lane assist and the Audi Pre-Sense Plus collision mitigation system) and the Black Optic package (available with the Sport package, and adds 20-inch wheels, summer tires and high-gloss black trim).

You're paying a little less for less room, but Audi isn't cutting any luxuries out of that tighter space.

7. 2016 BMW 535d

MSRP: $61,330

Miles per gallon: 29 city, 34 highway, 31.5 combined

BMW diesel is getting a closer look these days, but not here in the U.S. The 535 just received its diesel version in 2014, but BMW loaded it up with a standard navigation system, leather upholstery, adjustable driving settings (which alter suspension, steering, throttle and automatic transmission response), adaptive bi-xenon headlights, automatic wipers, heated mirrors, a sunroof, auto-dimming mirrors and keyless ignition/entry.

There's also dual-zone automatic climate control, dark wood trim, ambient lighting, ten-way power front seats, driver memory functions, split-folding rear seats, BMW Assist emergency communications system and the iDrive electronics interface (with smartphone app integration). A Premium upgrade includes a hands-free power trunk lid that opens or closes automatically when you swipe your foot under the rear bumper while holding the remote, keyless access to all doors and satellite radio.

6. 2015 Lexus GS 450h

MSRP: $61,330

Miles per gallon: 29 city, 34 highway, 31.5 combined

A sporty 306- to 336-horsepower hybrid with paddle shifters and a tachometer? Oh, Toyota's making it happen.

From the giant 12.3-inch display for navigation, audio and climate controls to voice command, a 17-speaker sound system, Bluetooth connectivity and the Lexus Enform information and entertainment app system, the GS 450h isn't short on toys. However, this hybrid makes its biggest impressions through heated and ventilated seats, 18-way power front seats, rear-seat audio and climate controls, a driver attention monitor that makes sure you aren't drifting and all-wheel drive that keeps you steady on slick roads. It isn't as if you're getting a discount price, but there's a whole lot of value built into this beast of a hybrid.

5. 2016 Mercedes Benz E250 Bluetec

MSRP: $52,650

Miles per gallon: 28 city, 42 highway, 35 combined

Feeling insecure about the diesel engine? An emergency monitoring service, a driver-fatigue monitor, pre-collision automatic braking, blind-spot detection and a lane-departure warning system should make you feel a bit more secure, as should the knowledge that Mercedes-Benz diesels haven't faced the same scrutiny as some of their counterparts.

That Mercedes engine and handling make the driver feel more like a racer than a chauffeur. Meanwhile the four-seater's COMAND entertainment, navigation and communication system, dual-zone automatic climate control, ventilated seats and rear DVD entertainment system should keep everyone else sated while their driver's at play.

4. 2016 Porsche Panamera E-Hybrid

MSRP: $96,100

Miles per gallon equivalent: 50 combined

Its 330-horsepower engine gets a 95-horsepower boost from the electric motor to push this Panamera from 0 to 60 in 5.2 seconds and tops out at 167 mph.

That's hybrid-aided performance of the type used in Formula One cars, even if Porsche frames it at a high-performance Prius. However, tossing in a 7-inch touchscreen entertainment and communications system, an 11-speaker base sound system ( which bumps up to 16 speakers and 1,000 watts if you pick the Burmester High-End system), navigation, voice control, HD Radio, Bluetooth, apps, rear-seat multimedia players, a telephone module, automatic climate control and noise and heat insulation all makes it a lot more plush than the average hybrid.


3. 2016 Mercedes-Benz S550e

MSRP: $95,650

Miles per gallon equivalent: 50 combined

For the same price as the 21.5-mpg base model, you can sacrifice the 4-liter V8 engine and 449 horsepower in favor of the more-efficient, hybrid-aided 3-liter, 329-horsepower V6. Let's be honest, here: you aren't going to be drag-racing your friends in a 550 anyway.

More likely, you're going to be playing with 16-way power massaging ventilated leather seats, multicolor/multizone LED lighting, the fragrance system in the vents, the rear-seat refrigerator box, the 4-zone automatic climate control, the dual 12.3-inch entertainment and information screens, the COMAND information and entertainment system with touchpad controller, a Burmester surround sound system, free navigation, global radio, dual USB ports, 10-gigabyte music hard drive, in-car wi-fi and rear seat entertainment system. If you have a car that's more focused on toys and creature comforts than it is on performance, you may as well conserve fuel while you enjoy the ride.

2. Mercedes-Benz B-Class electric drive

Starting price: $41,450

Miles per gallon equivalent: 85 city, 82 highway, 83.5 combined

Electric charge range: 87 miles

Yes, it's kind of on the small side and looks a bit like a luxury Nissan Leaf, but it generates 177 horsepower and has 43.6 cubic feet of cargo space with the seats down, power front seats, dual-zone automatic climate control, HD radio, Bluetooth streaming and a mobile app to keep track of its diagnostic information remotely. Right now, the B-Class electric drive is only available in 11 states (California, Connecticut, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New York, Oregon, Rhode Island and Vermont), but it's a low-end option for those seeking efficient Mercedes-Benz luxury. Unfortunately, most of the “luxury” is optional.

1. 2015 Tesla Model S

Starting price: $77,500 with incentives

Miles per gallon equivalent: 106 city, 95 highway, 100 combined

Electric charge range: 270 miles

If price is no object, this is the most mileage for your money. The 376-horsepower motor, 5.2-second 0-60 and 155 mph top speed all add up to more performance than any other electric vehicle can manage. Meanwhile, while not as efficient as the i3's 124 mpge, that 270-mile range, supercharger and eight-year, unlimited mile warranty are unmatched. With charger-oriented navigation in place and self-driving “autopilot” being implemented slowly but surely, this car's getting better just as the new Model X makes its way to market.
