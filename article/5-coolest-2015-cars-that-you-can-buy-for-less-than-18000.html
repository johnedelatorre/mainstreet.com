5 Coolest 2015 Cars That You Can Buy for Less Than $18,000

  • Jerry Kronenberg
  • May 21, 2015 8:15 AM EDT
ADVERTISEMENT

NEW YORK (MainStreet) — A super-sexy Bugatti Veyron sports car will cost you about $2.6 million, while a cutting-edge Tesla Model S electric vehicle will set you back some $70,000. But here's a look at some cool 2015 vehicles you can drive off of dealers' lots for less than $18,000.

"These cars all have a degree of coolness that belies their price. A lot of people will look at vehicles on the list and say: 'Wow, I'm surprised that car's not more expensive,'" says Jack Nerad of Kelley Blue Book, which recently released its 12th annual Coolest Cars Under $18,000 rundown.

KBB editors compile the subjective list by rating all models the firm calculates U.S. consumers are paying around $18,000 or less for on average.

Nerad says such budget vehicles have come a long way from their days of AM-only radios and hand-cranked windows.

"The level of equipment and sophistication in entry-level cars is so much greater than it used to be," he says. "This year's winners are practically limousines compared to the 'econoboxes' of the 1970s, '80s or '90s."

Experts say automakers pack lots of features into budget models these days partly because there's tons of competition for entry-level customers. "If you just put out an utterly vanilla car, you really can't sell it," Nerad says.

Joe Wiesenfelder of Cars.com adds that the government's Corporate Average Fuel Economy standards (known as "CAFE") require automakers to sell small, energy-efficient cars in addition to the big gas guzzlers that typically offer manufacturers the heftiest markups.

"If you want to sell high-profit pickup trucks and large SUVs, then you need to offset that with some things that are more efficient," he says. 

Read on to check out the five models Kelley Blue Book says are 2015's coolest low-priced cars. All dollar figures reflect KBB's "Fair Purchase Price" for each model's entry-level version. That's an estimate of what buyers can expect to pay for a given vehicle based on KBB's analysis of actual recent purchase prices.



Fifth-coolest: Honda Civic coupe
Fair Purchase Price: $17,267

Honda's fabled Civic combines a low sticker price with good performance, styling, reliability and standard features.

"The Civic coupe is probably the most fun to drive of any car in its class," Nerad says. "Having two doors also makes it better-looking than a lot of the competition, and it has all of the reliability that you'd expect from a Honda. That's a pretty compelling combination."

Base Civic coupes also come standard with amenities not usually seen at their price point, from a rearview camera to a six-speaker AM/FM/CD/USB/Bluetooth/Pandora audio system. Under the hood, entry-level models feature a 143-horsepower four-cylinder engine and manual transmission.



Fourth-coolest: Jeep Renegade
Fair Purchase Price: $18,402

The Renegade actually costs a tad more than $18,000, but KBB editors honored it anyway because of what Nerad calls the small crossover's "cool factor."

It's the only brand-new model on KBB's list, which Nerad says "is cool in and of itself." He adds that the iconic Jeep line "has its own cool imagery, while the Renegade's ability to go off-road is pretty cool, too."

Built on the same platform as a Fiat 500X, the Renegade offers some pretty impressive standard features, from dual sunroofs to power doors and windows (which aren't always included on entry-level vehicles).

Base Renegades also come with front-wheel drive, manual transmission and a four-cylinder turbocharged engine that cranks out a solid 160 horsepower. Or you can spend a little extra and upgrade to all-wheel drive, automatic transmission and a 180-horsepower I-4 engine.



Third-coolest: Kia Soul
Fair Purchase Price: $15,137
If this small wagon is cool enough for those hip Kia Soul Hamsters, it's certainly cool enough for you.

The Soul combines a low price with a trendy, boxy look and lots of Millennial-friendly features, such as a standard AM/FM/USB/Bluetooth/SiriusXM audio system.

"You can get a lot of equipment on a Soul and still pay no more than $18,000 — and it's a real looker of a car," Nerad says. "It certainly doesn't look like anything else out there, and that's always cool."



Second-coolest: Volkswagen Golf coupe
Fair Purchase Price: $17,608

The 2015 Golf has won two of the auto world's most-prestigious honors — the Motor Trend Car of Year award and the North American Car of the Year title. How cool is that?

Nerad says the Golf, which VW redesigned completely for 2015, offers "unparalleled styling and handling for a very low price. If you aspire to someday own something like a BMW 530 or Audi A4, the Golf will at least get you into that mindset for $18,000 or less."

The sporty hatchback's entry-level version also comes with some impressive standard features, such as audio system with eight speakers, touchscreen controls and SiriusXM capability. Additionally, base models combine manual transmission with a 170-horsepower turbocharged four-cylinder engine.



Coolest: Mazda Mazda3 Sedan
Fair Purchase Price: $16,681

The Mazda3 is so hip and affordable that it's made KBB's Coolest Cars for Under $18,000 list in all 12 years that the company has put out the annual rundown.

"The Mazda3 is a lot of fun to drive, has great interior accommodations and the styling is probably the best of any compact car," Nerad says.

Base models are somewhat spartan, but do come with a four-speaker AM/FM/USB stereo and power locks and windows. (Again, that's not always a given on low-priced cars.)

Mechanically, entry-level Mazda3s offer a 155-horsepower four-cylinder engine and manual transmission.

— Written by Jerry Kronenberg for MainStreet
