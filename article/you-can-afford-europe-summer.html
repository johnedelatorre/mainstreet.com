You Can Afford Europe This Summer

  • Robert McGarvey
  • May 8, 2014 8:39 AM EDT
ADVERTISEMENT

NEW YORK (MainStreet) — Don't get your hopes too high. You still cannot afford Paris or Rome or London.

But there is plenty more to Europe and numbers crunched by blogger Roger Wade for the 3 Star Traveler Index highlights top choices among attractive vacation destinations in Europe that can be enjoyed for under $100 per day.

What's that buy? A pretty decent holiday in fact. The hotel will be three star and in a "desirable" center city location. It will have "mostly positive" reviews. Wade also assumes that you have a travel companion who splits half the hotel cost.

Also in the budget: a couple taxi rides, entrance to a prime attraction (such as a museum), three meals per day (Wade said in an interview that he figures on dinner in a sit-down restaurant; breakfast and lunch will be fast food), and three drinks per day.

You could live like that?

The question is where. Know that some places you really want to visit make the list of top towns for cheapskates. There's a trending capital city, a drop dead gorgeous island, a richly historic city that straddles Europe and Asia, and a city filled with a past that may make you cry but is sure to thrill your soul. Any of them will reward a visit even when budget is no issue.

But some locales are, well, mainly just inexpensive.

Take the cheapest city on the list, Sofia, the capital of Bulgaria. Wade said that $48.72 will do you nicely per day there.

What's to do in Sofia? Uh...not much. As noted by Wade, "It's not easy to reach for most travelers and there is a lack of classic and famous sights in Sofia."

Other places unlikely to get many vacationers despite winning mention in the frugal top 10 are Riga, Latvia ($73.51 per day); Vilnius, Lithuania ($71.18 per day); and Bucharest, Romania ($51.25 daily).

A surprising number eight is St. Petersburg, Russia ($67.66) -- it has exquisite museums, a great food scene, even prime shopping - but, said Wade, although it is easy for cruise ship passengers to score day trip visas, it can be frustrating for other tourists to win a visa. By all means apply if the erstwhile Leningrad is on your bucket list. But know that the process is daunting and convoluted. Most countries really want tourist dollars. Russia is indifferent.

Russia also is why the #10 town on the list has to be x-ed out for all but the most intrepid: Kiev, in Ukraine at $75.78. It's a magnetic city, with a lot of draws for foreign visitors, but regional instability pushes it off most lists this year.

Tell you where to go?

Head to #17, Santorini, as pretty a Greek island as there is and an authentic bargain at $95.27 per the 3 Star Index. Overrun pretty much every summer day by cruise ship passengers, most nights the island empties out, and then it is yours to enjoy.

A word of advice: splurge on the local wine. And feta cheese. And olives. Let the Mediterranean sun beat down on you, stare at the waters that seem impossibly blue, and just know that you are in Greece.

If you have to overnight in Athens getting to Santorini - and you probably will - know that it ranks 20th on the list at $104.73. It is sweltering in summer (average daily high is 90 F in July), it may be overcrowded, but use the day to see the Acropolis and that will make it worth the detour.

You were hoping for more exotic? Istanbul, Turkey's major city, is $89.33 per day, placing it #14. It uniquely straddles east and west, Europe and Asia, and in much of the city center, wherever you look there is history - the Holy Roman Empire, the Ottoman Empire, the Cold War.

Stroll through the Grand Bazaar - some 3,000 shops are in this ancient marketplace. Buy Turkish wine - surprisingly good, always cheap.

You want more, well, European? Number three on the list is Hungary's capital, Budapest, at $56.60 per day.

A "fantastic bargain," said Wade.

Eat goulash, walk the grand streets of the city center, and know that this has been an important capital for centuries, going back to the Habsburgs when it was an anchor in that lavish empire.

Budapest lately has become a hotspot for youth, who have tired of Berlin, and who are in search of the next "in" place.

Get there now, before the crowd.

Want one more city to consider? Wade offered his personal favorite: Krakow, in Poland, #5 at $65.99 per day. "The best bargain in Europe," he said.

Krakow has been 1,000 years in the making. Now Poland's second biggest city, it was the capital for a few hundred years many centuries ago. In World War II, the Nazis sought to preserve the city - the plan was to populate it with Germans - but the other part of the plan was exterminating the Polish and Jewish populations. Many died in Krakow, and that sadness is plain fact.

But Krakow today is a city on the rebound and, said Wade, if you can go, go. "Krakow should be on the list of Europe's top 10 destinations, not among the five cheapest," he said.

But it is and that makes it a don't miss for 2014.
