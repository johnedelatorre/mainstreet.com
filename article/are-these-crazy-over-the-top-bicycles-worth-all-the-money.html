Are These Crazy, Over-the-Top Bicycles Worth All the Money?

  • Mia Taylor
  • Apr 7, 2015 7:00 AM EDT
ADVERTISEMENT

NEW YORK (MainStreet) — When spending nearly $50,000 on a bicycle, it may be more appropriate to hang it on the wall like a piece of artwork than ride it on the street.

This past weekend in Monte Carlo, LikeBike showcased some of the most over-the-top, expensive models hitting the market to those well-heeled enough to buy — since the bikes on display at this first-ever show can cost as much as your Honda, or a down payment on a house.

Among the top 10 most expensive bikes at the show was a Malle Bicyclette by Monyat that cost $49,000, a Bestianera Montecarlo Edition by T°RED for about $19,500 and a Spinas by Nevi retailing for around $14,000. 

Wladimir Singer, co-founder of LikeBike, says the show highlighted the latest in electric bicycles, smart bicycles (which combine electronics and GPS) and custom bicycles.

"It's a very exciting show," says Singer in a telephone interview from Monte Carlo. "It's a platform to exhibit the exceptional bikes being released and also the evolution of bikes."

"We tried to combine the luxury of bicycling today with innovation, design and ecology," he says. "But also to make this show fun for the consumer."

But while these bikes may be fun to look at and daydream about, you don't have to spend tens of thousands to enjoy the latest luxuries and technological advancements in bicycling today.

"These expensive bikes are built to have a wow effect and attract press for the companies making them. You usually see these bikes in museums," says Uli Fluhme, co-founder of Campagnolo Gran Fondo New York, a 100-mile road cycling marathon from New York City to Bear Mountain that draws athletes from more than 70 countries — high-end riders, many of whom work in finance and enjoy riding the best bikes available. "Nowadays, you get more then ever for $1,000 to $2,000. You don't need to spend $15,000 on a bike."

Fluhme says to check out De Rosa or Colnago bicycles if you're considering spending a substantial sum. Both are Italian manufacturers known for using exceptional components.

"De Rosa does high-end bikes with the best components. It is something that wins the Tour de France," he says.

Meanwhile, Dave Adornato, owner of Montclair Bikery in Montclair, N.J., compares the purchase of a Cipollini RB1000 Luxury Edition for $54,000 or a Sarto 18K for $29,400 (both on display in Monte Carlo) to the purchase of a sports car for the pro racing athlete.

"You are buying a tool that is utilized by people who are winning pro-level races," says Adornato, who sells maybe one to two $15,000 bikes each year. "With the Cipollini, not only is the frame an exotic carbon fiber design, but it's paired with some of best technology out there. It also comes with electronic shifting."

"Although these prices are high for a bicycle, when you compare it to other sports, these bikes allow an individual to experience the sport at a level the average person couldn't normally experience in other disciplines, such as auto racing. So I compare it to buying a $100,000 race car," Adornato says.

While pro bikes may cost in the tens of thousands of dollars, Adornato says many companies are now creating scaled-down versions, both in terms of design and pricing, allowing the average consumer to buy an approximation of the professional bikes for thousands less.

Some of the companies worth noting, he says, are Specialized and Trek.

"The consumer gets the benefit of the technology used to develop pro bikes for just $3,000," he says.

Some of the newest technology appearing in bikes at all ends of the price spectrum includes electronic shifting (as opposed to the old-fashioned mechanical shifting and cables). Companies are also pushing the envelope when it comes to the use of carbon fiber in manufacturing, making them lighter then ever and more aerodynamic.

Mila Pantovich, lifestyle editor for Justluxe.com, has been following the luxury biking industry for years as well. She too has some recommendations for those seeking to look stylish while pedaling around town without breaking the bank.

For the fashion-conscious, Pantovich recommends checking out Mosi Bicycles or Peace Bicycles.

"Peace bicycles are a neat company. Their bikes give you a nice retro look, like what was seen among the classic 1970s hip kids," she says. "They are for more fashion-forward, for people who want to be seen."

Prices for the Peace and Mosi bikes are much more wallet friendly: around $700 to $800, Pantovich said.

As for the ultra-luxurious bikes on display in Monte Carlo, Pantovich says you might be surprised by who is snatching them up.

"It's actually a bigger range of people than you might think buying these bikes," Pantovich says. "From the kind of person who wants a visual signifier of their wealth to the person who collects art — to the real cycling enthusiast."

Here are some of the most expensive bikes that were on display in Monte Carlo:

Malle Bicyclette by Monyat
Price: $49,000 (made to order)
Monyat was founded in 1849 by Parisian Pauline Monyat, the only female trunk maker in history. Its Malle Bicyclette is said to be the perfect bike for a picnic — it comes with porcelain goblets as well as plates and cutlery - and at nearly $50,000 ought to be.

Bestianera Montecarlo Edition by T°RED
Price: $19,500
This bike's claim to fame is that it is the lightest, most flexible bike ever. The Montecarlo Edition also lets you switch from a track bike to a hybrid sport-urban bike in less than 10 minutes.

Bicicletto by SPA
Price: $13,074
Bicicletto is an Italian design icon. The Bicicletto is an electric bike that offers innovative design and an electronic throttle that allows you to ride without even pedaling.

Pininfarina Fuoriserie by Pininfarina
Price: $9,800
The Pininfarina Fuoriserie is a tailor-made bike from Italy, crafted by artisans. Only 30 will be made. Inspired by 1930s bespoke cars, the bike has an extremely detailed saddle and handlebars with woven leather. It also has a micro-electric engine to make your ride even smoother.


Aero Road 6.2 by Dassi
Price: $8,122
If you are a fan of Rolls-Royce, this may be the bike for you. Designed by an former Rolls-Royce engineer, the bike is hand-painted and assembled in the U.K. 

Urban One by Guapa
Price: $7,282
A bespoke handcrafted wooden bike, this creation truly is worthy of hanging on your wall. And only a handful of people will be able to do so, as only five of these bikes are being released as part of the company's first production. Designed and made in England, the bicycle combines cutting-edge technology with timeless design. More of the bikes will be available to order in the future, with bespoke custom spray and engraving options.
