Here Are 5 Great Destinations for Winter Dark Week Travel

  • Jason Notte
  • Jan 4, 2016 4:55 PM EST
ADVERTISEMENT

It doesn't matter if an airport in much of January through February is basically a spa with jetways: there's no way you're going back there if it was your personal gulag in December.

It's that post-holiday travel trauma that makes the last three weeks of January and first few weeks of February "dark weeks" for the travel industry. You'll see airlines and hotels pull out all the stops to draw customers who've been scared off airports for a good, long time by hours of delays, wailing infants and subpar food ordered off of germ-laden touchscreens. These are desperate times for a travel industry that's basically asking consumers to relive a holiday nightmare before they've had chance to heal.

Last year, 43% of travelers surveyed by TripAdvisor said they expected to be stressed out by their winter holiday trips. That doesn't include the 31% of travelers who were already reduced to emotional rubble by Thanksgiving travel. That stress wasn't helped by the cost of those trips -- estimated at an average of $436 per person -- which were up from $401 in 2013 according to Trip Advisor. Finally, the lack of vacation days accrued by many American workers this early in the year -- and the fact that we're one of the few Western economies that doesn't mandate paid vacation time -- keeps much of the U.S. at home this time of year.

That yields a whole lot of empty hotel rooms, vacant airports and nonexistent tourists. About 50 million passengers took trips aboard U.S. airlines last January, according to the Department of Transportation's Bureau of Transportation statistics. That's up almost 2 million passengers from January 2013, but still well below the 55.5 million passengers who caught holiday flights a month earlier in December and woefully shy of the 65.1 million who fly during peak season in July.

There is only one month in which travelers get around less. In February 2015, 47.2 million passengers paid for their cheap trips in terrible weather and lengthy delays.

For travelers who either kept things low-key during the holiday season or have just one more big trip left in them, this is a great time to catch a deal. We consulted with the folks at TripAdvisor's travel deals site SmarterTravel and found five places that make for great “dark week” travel destinations:

New York

The Rockettes left on January 3, the Rockefeller Center tree comes down on January 9 and the overwhelming majority of holiday tourists were swept out of Times Square with the confetti.

As a New Yorker, this is either one of your favorite times of year, because the tourists aren't crowding your walk to work, restaurant reservations are plentiful and theater tickets have returned to somewhat reasonable prices or one of the worst, because you're about to spend the next few weeks shoveling or sliding. If you're want to visit the city during its coldest, least hospitable time of year, however, you have plenty of impetus to do so.

Sure, Wollman Rink at Central Park and the Winter Village at Bryant Park are still open, but it's the hotel prices that make New York so inviting right now. Average nightly hotel rates from January through March hover around $235 a night, or 22% lower than the rest of the year. Kimpton Hotels offers rooms from $170 a night from January through March 21. If you're too late for that, Hotel Week New York City runs now through January 15 and comes with steep discounts. Meanwhile, the city's Restaurant Week (January 18 through February 5), offers three-course lunches for $25 and dinners for $38, while two-for-one theatre tickets on select shows await during NYC Broadway Week (January 19 through February 5) and NYC Off-Broadway Week (February 1 through 14).

Tulum, Mexico

While this spot near Cancun gets busy during the holiday season, it dies down significantly by the end of January into February. If you wanted to sneak some time in before spring break, you were served well by waiting until after the holidays, when some of the better promotions go into effect.

The closest major airport to Tulum is Cancun (about a two-hour drive), so you'll find cheap Cancun-bound flights, but head to a far more secluded location. Frontier, Spirit, and United all offer cheap round-trip tickets from major U.S. cities., and SmarterTravel points to Diamante K and Posada Yum Kin for deep discounts.

Southern California

The bowl games are over, the holiday Disneyland crowds are gone and the tourists are generally avoiding both Los Angeles and San Diego until the spring.

However, as pods of grey whales migrate from Alaska to Mexico for the winter, San Diego with lots of discounts for whale-watching trips during the next few weeks. Even if you're on the East Coast, flights to both L.A. and San Diego run for less than $500 round-trip, with JetBlue offering particularly cheap fares. The major hotel chains' deals for both San Diego and Los Angeles aren't shabby, either.

Barbados

For reasons that should be particularly obvious to anyone shaking a fist at snowy forecasts around this time of year, it's peak travel season in the Caribbean.

However, Barbados is typically cost-prohibitive thanks to its relative dearth of flights compared to other Caribbean locations. However, a new direct route from Boston on JetBlue just started in November and helped decrease flight prices to the island. Flights are less than $200 each way from the East Coast after the holiday season, though hotel deals are a bit tougher to come by. SmarterTravel notes the free resort credits being offered at Bouganvillea Beach Resort, but most of your savings are going to come from the flight itself.

Austin, Texas

Longhorns football has packed it in for the season, and South By Southwest doesn't kick off until March: if you aren't into throngs of people, there's just about no better time to be here.

Peak season comes with SXSW in March, so package deals can be found during the winter months, when temperatures remain in the 60s and 70s. Barton Spring Pool stays 68 degrees year round, and the city's golf courses are seeing less demand for tee times. All the beer, barbecue and live music is yours for the taking but only if you can get there before the end of February. As SmarterTravel discovered, airfare to Austin from most major U.S. cities averages under $200 round-trip when a bunch of tech bros, film students and music geeks aren't scrambling to get from concert to keynote to party.
