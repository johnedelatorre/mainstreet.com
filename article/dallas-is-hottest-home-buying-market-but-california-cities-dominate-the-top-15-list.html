Dallas Is Hottest Home Buying Market But California Cities Dominate The Top 15 List

  • Juliette Fairley
  • May 15, 2015 5:50 AM EDT
ADVERTISEMENT

NEW YORK (MainStreet) — The homebuying season is off and running, setting off higher prices in 15 of the hottest medium to large sized markets in the country for both buyers and sellers.

“As the market shows strong signs of health and improvement during the start of spring home buying season, these 15 markets are the best in the country from both a supply and demand perspective,” said Jonathan Smoke, chief economist with Realtor.com.

Leading the list is Dallas, according to Realtor.com’s ranking.

“Dallas is emerging as the top market after years of fighting for that title with Houston,” Smoke told MainStreet.

Following behind Dallas are areas in and around Santa Rosa, Calif.; Vallejo, Calif.; Denver and Boston.

“Sellers are seeing listings move between 24 and 49 days more quickly in these cities than in the rest of the country and at an accelerating pace from just last month, an average of five days faster,” Smoke said.

How quickly a home sells is one way to measure the popularity of a metropolis, and although home listings in Dallas nabbed the top spot as especially attractive to buyers based on number of views on Realtor.com, cities in the state of California dominated the list. In addition to the aforementioned, they include San Diego, San Francisco, Santa Cruz, San Luis Obispo, Ventura and Sacramento.

“Housing in California is the fastest moving inventory in terms of lowest median days on the market,” said Smoke. “On average, housing in San Jose sells in 24 days.”

Overall, the cost of housing is on the rise nationwide. List prices increased to $225,000, up 9% from this time last year, according to Realtor.com.

“An apartment or home in New York or Silicon Valley is much more expensive than in Greensboro, North Carolina,” said Steve Lowisz, CEO with Qualigence International, an employment firm. “If employers want to move someone to a higher cost area, they often have to pay them more, thus inflating salaries and operating costs.”

Denver scored fourth on the list due to multiple industries located there, including legalized recreational marijuana.

“Many of these industries are doing extremely well in today’s economy, such as aviation and aerospace, healthcare, technology and software,” Smoke said.

One dark horse on the list is Detroit -- due to a lack of skilled employment that would allow for the sorts of salaries that allow people to become homeowners.

“Many of the skilled trades and line jobs once associated with the automotive industry disappeared after the recession,” Lowisz told MainStreet. “This caused a large percentage of the local population who did not initially have other skills to take lower paying jobs. With such a high level of demand of employees seeking employment, employers could pay less due to the competition.”

Other cities ranking in the top 15 are Nashville, Boston, Ann Arbor and Boulder.

“It’s great to see Detroit make the list as the market is emerging strongly from more than a decade of decline,” said Smoke. “Nashville isn’t a surprise, but it’s presence on the list may surprise others because it is emerging as one of the strongest in the South. Boston is the sole representative in the Northeast and its strong economy is shaking off the historically harsh winter.”

—Written for MainStreet by Juliette Fairley
