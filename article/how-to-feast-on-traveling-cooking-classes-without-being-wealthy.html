How to Feast on Traveling Cooking Classes Without Being Wealthy

  • Mia Taylor
  • Apr 17, 2015 7:00 AM EDT
ADVERTISEMENT

NEW YORK (MainStreet) — Regent Seven Seas Cruises recently announced plans to launch the most luxurious cruise ship in the world, a floating palace that will include such things as a 3,875-square-foot suite, extravagantly designed lounges and lavish gourmet restaurants.

The ship, to take its maiden voyage next year, will also be home to a stunning cooking school reminiscent of France's most prestigious culinary academies.

The ship's classroom has 18 individual cooking stations atop long, curved white quartzite countertops accented with stainless steel. Each is equipped with top-of-the-line induction cooktops, stainless steel sinks and a comprehensive collection of cooking essentials.

But the most breathtaking thing about the class is its view. Floor to ceiling windows give participants an intoxicating view of the ocean while learning about food and wine pairings, fish and shellfish preparation and delectable mini-desserts.

Making this particular culinary learning experience even more unique: Ingredients for class meals and meals themes will be based on the ports the ship visits, including Colombia, Nicaragua, Costa Rica and Guatemala.

Learning to be the next Giada De Laurentiis or Wolfgang Puck in this elegant setting, though, comes at a price. A 14-night voyage aboard the all-suite ship starts at a steep $7,499, and that doesn't even include the cooking classes. 

But you don't need to spend a small fortune to participate in high-quality cooking lessons in fantastic locations. There are classes all over the globe that won't break your budget. Your biggest expense will be traveling to the destination.

"I've found that the most expensive classes aren't necessarily the best classes," says Laura Goyer, publisher of the website The Culinary Travel Guide. "The problem with having an expensive class is that the more you charge, the higher the expectations are."

Goyer, a food and travel writer who specializes in affordable, authentic culinary travel, has taken cooking classes around the world.

Her approach is to seek out classes on vacations she was planning to take anyway, including in France and Italy.

"Taking a cooking class while traveling is an opportunity to immerse yourself in the culture through food," Goyer says. "And if you take the cooking class at the beginning of your trip somewhere, then you can get all kinds of great information about good restaurants and shopping. Plus, you sit down and have dinner with all of the people in your cooking class after you've prepared the meal, so it turns into a dinner party. To me, that's the main appeal. I do learn things about cooking. But I don't necessarily come home and try and recreate the recipes."

Asia's cooking classes are even more budget friendly, as food and travel blogger Katy Bloxsom of Colorado found stumbling across one while on a monthlong visit to Vietnam.

For just $43 per person, Katy and her husband Nick participated in a cooking class on an organic farm outside Ho Chi Minh City. 

"It was really incredible. They picked us up from where we were staying, and when we arrived there, they had welcome drinks, a beautiful outdoor kitchen, hammocks, and the farm was gorgeous," Bloxsom says. "It was a very small class — just six of us and the chef."

She found similar inexpensive opportunities in Greece, where a food and wine class cost about $133 per person.

"For me, food and culture go hand in hand, they are so linked," Bloxsom says. "And I really want to experience the culture through the food and learn about why dishes are important to cultures ... It's really valuable as a tourist."

Here are five surprisingly budget-friendly cooking classes in exotic locations:

The Enrica Rocca Cooking School in Venice, Italy

Ever wanted to peek inside a palazzo in Venice? Here's your chance. These cooking classes take place inside Rocca's family palazzo on a side street in the Dorsoduro sestiere section of Venice.

Gourmet magazine lists Rocca's offering as one of the 10 best cooking schools in the world. Its most expensive class, a full day, is about $350.

Some of Rocca's classes include a market tour, during which you get to pick out the food that will be cooked.

Le Cordon Bleu School, France

The name of this world-famous institution seems to promise priciness, but classes here are less then $100, Goyer says.

"This is where Julia Child went and got her start," says Goyer, who has taken several classes at the school. "They have all kinds of classes, too — classes where you can just watch and some that are hands on, some pastry classes and some savory classes and some that include market tours too. They are great."

If you're planning a trip to France, Goyer advises reserving your space through the school's website as soon as you have your trip dates secured. Classes fill quickly.

Peter Island Resort & Spa's Grilling and Smoking Class on the Beach

This class presents another ocean view experience while learning to cook. In this case, Executive Chef Lisa Sellars guides guests through her grilling and smoking class beachside on a private island resort in the British Virgin Islands.

In addition to hands-on grilling and smoking lessons, the class includes mixology lessons and a walk through the resort's herb and vegetable garden, where additional ingredients for meals are gathered.

"We talk about the herbs and how they influence the fish and meat," Sellars says. "Then we go down to beach and learn the difference between dry rubs and wet rubs, the difference between using charcoal and wood, and learn about different ingredients we use on the island — like mango seeds and coconut husks and how they affect grilling and smoking."

The class is $100 per person if you are on the resort's meal plan. For those not on the meal plan, the cost is $160.

"It's a luxury to come here," Sellars says of the island. "And once you are here, it's a luxury to be able to cook in this environment, on the beach."

Ho Chi Minh Cooking Class, Vietnam

These unique and healthy classes teach you how to cook, but also how to use food as medicine. The $43 class, which takes place on a farm, includes tours of a Vietnamese medicine garden and a mushroom house.

"We learned about different flavor combinations, talked a lot about balancing sweet, sour and spicy in a dish," Bloxsom says. "We went on a tour of the farm, picked all of our fruits and vegetables, and talked about the different health benefits of herbs."

The Mandarin Oriental, Bangkok, Legendary Cooking School

A leader among hotel cooking schools, Bangkok's Mandarin Oriental offers one-off classes and series to learn the art of Thai cooking. Classes also include historical and cultural details, demonstrations and hands-on participation.

Some of the mouth-watering lessons here include sweet and sour fish soup, leaf-wrapped mackerel and sticky rice dumplings. Courses cost as little as $82.

— Written by Mia Taylor for MainStreet
