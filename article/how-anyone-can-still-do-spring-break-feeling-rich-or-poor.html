How Anyone Can Still Do Spring Break, Feeling Rich or Poor

  • Mia Taylor
  • Apr 3, 2015 7:00 AM EDT
ADVERTISEMENT

NEW YORK (MainStreet) — Calling all golf fans: The first Tiger Woods-designed golf course in the world is officially open for business. 

The course is just one reason the sunny, come-hither destination of Los Cabos, Mexico, should be on the public's radar for spring break this year.

Struck by Hurricane Odile in September 2014, Cabo has done an amazing job of recovering and is now among the top spring break destinations for 2015, according to industry influencers that include Kayak.com, HipMunk.com and American Express Travel.

While Cabo has always been popular - particularly among celebrities - there's been a surge in visitor interest attributable to numerous factors, including that it truly offers something for all budgets.

"You can find hotels from $90, usually business hotels such as Marriott or Hampton Inn, or you can find luxurious accommodations that are nowhere else in Latin America. And there's also very nice properties with all-inclusive plans," says Luis Palacios, commercial director for the Los Cabos Tourism Board.

Nearly 11,000 of the destination's 14,000 hotel rooms are back online as part of the rebuilding and recovery effort. Hotel owners have invested serious amounts of cash in making Cabo even better then it was pre-hurricane.

"A lot of the hotels were completely renovated," Palacios says. "The One & Only Palmilla invested more than $140 million just in renovations. And that's only one example. Not only are hotels being renovated, but also new rooms are being added. In the coming months and years we will have a Hampton Inn & Suites in 2015, a JW Marriott, also in 2015, and a Hard Rock Hotel in 2016. We also keep hearing that there will be a Ritz-Carlton Reserve for 2017."

Translation: Cabo is becoming hotter then ever.

If you haven't booked your spring break vacation for 2015 yet, it's not too late. Many hotels and destinations continue offering special deals through the end of April, and sometimes into early May.

Here's a rundown of some of the hot destinations to consider this year, for all ends of the budget spectrum:

Europe 

The euro and British pound are at an all-time low against the U.S. dollar, making now a great time to visit Europe if you're working within a budget.

For families with young children in tow, the Rocco Forte's Hotel de Rome in Berlin offers an interesting option.

Its concierge team created an interactive program to engage children and teenagers in history and culture, with activities including bike tours along the trail of The Wall, a graffiti workshop, discovering underground bunkers, exploring parks and gardens, watching animals in the zoo at feeding time, taking DJ lessons or painting a Royal Prussian porcelain plate. 

And parents will like this: Children under 3 get complimentary food and beverages, and a 50% reduction is granted for kids between 3 and 12 years old.

The hotel also offers special kids' menus, junior bath time accessories, games to borrow, "splash hour" in the pool with water toys and an invitation to the bar for a mocktail mixology class. All are part of the hotel's "Family Affair" package.

Hotel de Rome's family package rate starts at around $612 per night for two deluxe rooms inclusive of breakfast and VAT. The hotel offers a spring promotion that is 25% off the regular rate (with a minimum stay of three nights) and starts at $305 per night in a classic room inclusive of breakfast and VAT (double occupancy).

Central America

File this option squarely under "splurges."

The destination? Panama City, Panama. The accommodations - $3,500 per night for a stay in the mega villa known as Casa Naga, which was recently unveiled by The Westin Playa Bonita. The 25,000-square-foot villa overlooks the rainforest and Pacific Ocean. And if lounging around this stunning home enjoying its remarkable views is not enough, there's always plenty to do in Panama City. From the Miraflores Locks to the Caribbean beaches of Isla Grande, Panama is quickly becoming a Central American gem.

Florida
Located on Duck Key in the Middle Florida Keys, the 60-acre Hawks Cay Resort, Villas and Marina is home to nearly every water-based recreation under the sun. 

The resort just completed a multimillion-dollar renovation that touched nearly every element of the property, including the Coral Cay kids' club. The centerpiece of the upgraded Coral Cay is a pirate ship pool that includes slides, tunnels and water-shooting cannons. 

The property's Camp Hawk, for kids ages 5 through 12, offers organized snorkeling, scavenger hunts and dockside fish feedings as well a nature-focused camp with animal touch tanks, nature arts and crafts and games.

While the kids are off having fun, parents can enjoy plenty of their own activities. 

"The Keys have so much to offer. People don't realize that it's a great location for an adventure vacation," says Andrew Rosuck, regional vice president of sales and marketing for Hawks Cay Resort. "There is all kinds of unbelievable fishing excursions, or you can see alligators and sharks here. You can go scuba diving, snorkeling or even snuba - which is the big up-and-coming activity. It's a cross between snorkeling and scuba."

A bonus when vacationing in the Keys for spring break, Rosuck says, is the break from the crowds.

The prices at Hawks Cay resort range from around $500 per night for a deluxe room to $750 a night for a two- to three-bedroom villa. The property's penthouse suite costs about $1,000 per night.

As part of the recent renovations, there are now 16 new family suites at the resort that include bunk beds for the kids.

Cabo

Here are a few hotel options from each category in Cabo's booming hotel industry:

On the super luxurious end of the spectrum there is the One & Only Palmilla, where rates start at $455 per night. The hotel also offers the "Villa Cortez," which accommodates eight adults and their children for $11,000 per night.

Another high-end family friendly favorite is the Casa Dorado, where a one-week stay in a junior suite is about $4,097.

Among the family-friendly all-inclusive resorts in Cabo is the Fiesta Americana Los Cabos, where a one-week double occupancy runs around $3,311.

 — Written by Mia Taylor for MainStreet
